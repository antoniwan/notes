**Send Help** asks a rude question early: what if surviving the crash is the part the movie skips past with a joke, because the real trap is who you’re stuck with? The premise is lean—two colleagues, an island, baggage in every sense—and the film leans on Raimi’s taste for glee and dread sharing the same shot.

Rachel McAdams and Dylan O’Brien sell the brittle chemistry that keeps the first act funny without feeling cute. The comedy lands because it’s threaded with impatience and fear; once the story tightens, the laughs don’t vanish so much as they curdle. That shift is the reason I’ll remember this one: not as a twist factory, but as a mood engine that keeps turning the screw.

I’m holding details light on purpose. If you like survival stories that let the camera enjoy the discomfort, watch the trailer, then save the rest for a big screen if you can—the sound design and blocking deserve room to breathe.
